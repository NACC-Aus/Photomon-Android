See your environment change through the eye of a camera.

One of the simplest and unambiguous ways to detect environmental change is by taking regular photos of exactly the same place over time. Photo-monitoring, as it is known, can be used to document change in absolutely anything you wish to photograph, from seasonal growth of algae in a rock pool to the effects of grazing on vegetation over a broad, sweeping landscape. And the great thing is, anyone can do it!

Photo-monitoring provides an accurate record of environmental change if :

  • Every photo from a monitoring point has the same the field-of-view;
  • Photos are taken regularly and at a frequency suitable for capturing the information you want to record;
  • The resulting photos are easily stored and secure, yet readily accessible to those who can benefit most from the information.

The photo-monitoring app you have installed was developed by the Northern Agricultural Catchments Council (NACC) to assist community volunteers to monitor beach profile change in Geraldton, Western Australia. It incorporates features aimed at simplifying the photo-monitoring process and increasing its accuracy. We hope you find it fun and useful for your own photo-monitoring needs.

NACC is the regional natural resource management organisation for the Northern Agricultural Region of Western Australia (WA), based in Geraldton. This app was funded by WA’s Department of Planning through the Coastwest program and the federal governments’ Caring for our Country.
